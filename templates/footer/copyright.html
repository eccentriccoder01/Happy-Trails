Fair Use Guidelines

Under certain circumstances, limited use of copyrighted material may qualify as "fair use":

Fair Use Factors
  • Purpose & Character: Is it for education, criticism, commentary, or news reporting? Non-commercial and transformative uses are more likely to be fair use.
  • Nature of Work: Using factual or published works is more likely to be fair use than using creative or unpublished works.
  • Amount Used: Using small portions is more likely to be fair use. Using the "heart" of the work makes it less likely.
  • Market Effect: If your use doesn't harm the market value or potential market for the original work, it's more likely to be fair use.

Examples of Potential Fair Use:

  • Quoting brief excerpts in a review or critique
  • Using screenshots for educational tutorials
  • Citing content in academic research papers
  • Commentary or parody that transforms the original
  • News reporting with proper attribution
Disclaimer

This is general information only, not legal advice. Fair use is complex and determined on a case-by-case basis. When in doubt, seek permission or consult a legal professional.
