Understanding how we use cookies to enhance your Happy Trails experience

Last Updated: November 3, 2025

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

At Happy Trails, we use cookies to enhance your browsing experience, remember your preferences, and understand how you interact with our website. This helps us improve our services and provide you with a personalized experience.

Key Points About Cookies
  • Small & Harmless: Cookies are tiny text files that cannot harm your device
  • No Personal Data: Cookies don't contain personally identifiable information by default
  • You're in Control: You can manage or delete cookies at any time through your browser settings
  • Industry Standard: Almost all websites use cookies to improve user experience

Cookies help us remember your login status, language preferences, items in your booking cart, and other settings. Without cookies, you would have to set your preferences every time you visit our website.

First-Party vs Third-Party Cookies

It's important to understand the difference between first-party and third-party cookies:

First-Party Cookies
Set directly by Happy Trails on happytrails.vercel.app domain. We have full control over these cookies and use them to improve your experience on our website.
Third-Party Cookies
Set by external services like Google Analytics or advertising partners. These help us analyze traffic and show relevant ads across the internet.

How We Use Cookies

Happy Trails uses cookies for the following purposes:

  1. Authentication & Security: To keep you logged in securely and protect your account from unauthorized access. Essential cookies remember your login session and verify your identity.
  2. User Preferences: To remember your language choice, display settings, route favorites, and other personalized preferences so you don't have to set them every visit.
  3. Booking Process: To maintain items in your booking cart, remember your selected routes, and facilitate the ticket purchase process across multiple pages.
  4. Website Performance: To analyze how visitors use our website, identify popular features, detect errors, and optimize loading times for a better user experience.
  5. Personalization: To provide personalized content recommendations, suggest relevant routes based on your travel history, and customize your homepage experience.
  6. Marketing & Analytics: To understand the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns, track conversions, and show you relevant advertisements on third-party websites.
Important Note

Disabling certain types of cookies may impact your ability to use some features of our website. For example, disabling functional cookies may prevent the site from remembering your language preference or login status.

Cookie Duration & Data Retention

Cookies can be classified based on how long they stay on your device:

Session Cookies
Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. Used for essential functions like maintaining your login session.
Persistent Cookies
Remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted. Used to remember your preferences across multiple visits.

Data Retention Policy

We retain cookie data for the following durations:

  • Essential Cookies: Session only (deleted when browser closes)
  • Functional Cookies: Up to 1 year or until you change preferences
  • Analytics Cookies: Up to 2 years (Google Analytics standard)
  • Marketing Cookies: 30-90 days depending on campaign duration
Automatic Cleanup

All cookies automatically expire after their retention period. You can also manually delete cookies at any time through your browser settings or by clicking "Clear All Cookies" in our preference center.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about cookies and privacy:

What happens if I disable all cookies?
If you disable all cookies, you won't be able to use essential features of our website, including logging in, making bookings, or maintaining items in your cart. We recommend keeping essential cookies enabled and customizing other cookie categories based on your preferences.
Are cookies safe? Can they harm my device?
Yes, cookies are completely safe! They are simple text files that cannot execute code, install malware, or harm your device in any way. Cookies can only store data provided by the website or your browser, and they cannot access other files on your computer.
Can cookies track my browsing on other websites?
First-party cookies set by Happy Trails can only track your activity on our website. However, third-party cookies (like those from Google Analytics or advertising networks) can track your browsing across multiple websites. You can disable third-party cookies through our preference center or your browser settings.
How often should I clear my cookies?
There's no need to regularly clear cookies unless you're troubleshooting website issues or want to reset your browsing data. Cookies automatically expire after their set duration. However, if you share your device with others or use a public computer, clearing cookies after each session is a good security practice.
Do cookies work the same way on mobile devices?
Yes, cookies work similarly on mobile browsers. However, mobile apps may use different tracking technologies like device IDs or SDKs. When you use Happy Trails through a mobile browser, the same cookie policy applies. You can manage cookies in your mobile browser settings just like on desktop.
What's the difference between cookies and tracking pixels?
Cookies are text files stored on your device, while tracking pixels are tiny invisible images embedded in web pages or emails. Both can track user behavior, but pixels don't require user action to work. Happy Trails primarily uses cookies and is transparent about any tracking technologies we employ.
Will my preferences sync across devices?
Cookie preferences are stored locally on each device and don't automatically sync. If you're logged in to your Happy Trails account, some preferences (like favorite routes) may sync across devices, but cookie consent choices need to be set separately on each device and browser.
How can I contact you about cookie concerns?
We take your privacy concerns seriously! You can contact our Privacy Team at privacy@happytrails.com for any questions about cookies, data protection, or privacy. We typically respond within 48 hours during business days.
